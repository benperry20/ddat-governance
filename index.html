Let's Follow an Idea Through Governance

We'll trace a real example: HR wants to implement a Learning Management System (LMS) to improve staff training and development.

THE IDEA

üìö "We need a Learning Management System to track employee training, deliver online courses, and ensure compliance with mandatory certifications."

üí°

Stage 1: Idea Identified

A business need surfaces that might require technology

üë§ Who's Involved

  • You (the business stakeholder)
  • Your manager or director
  • Colleagues who share the need

üìã What Happens

  • You identify a pain point or opportunity
  • You think "there must be a technology solution"
  • You might have already found a product online

‚ùì Questions at This Stage

  • What problem am I trying to solve?
  • Who else has this same need?
  • Is this aligned with our department's priorities?
üí°

Key Insight

Don't go straight to procurement or start shopping for solutions! Your next step is to connect with your assigned Business Relationship Manager (BRM).

ü§ù

Stage 2: Meet with Your BRM

Your Business Relationship Manager helps shape the idea

üë§ Who's Involved

  • You (the requester)
  • Your assigned BRM from ITS
  • Possibly your manager

üìã What Happens

  • BRM listens to understand the business need
  • Checks if existing solutions might work
  • Identifies if others have similar needs
  • Helps frame the opportunity properly

‚ùì Questions Your BRM Will Ask

  • What's the business problem you're solving?
  • How are you handling this today?
  • Who else would benefit from this?
  • Is this in your business plan or budget?
‚úì

In Our Example

The BRM confirms the LMS need is already in the HR Strategy. They note that Dayforce (existing HR system) has some training features. They'll help develop this into a formal concept for intake.

üìù

Stage 3: PMO Intake

The idea becomes a documented concept with a business case

üë§ Who's Involved

  • ITS Project Management Office
  • You and your BRM
  • Business subject matter experts

üìã What Happens

  • Idea documented in standard format
  • Business case developed
  • High-level requirements captured
  • Preliminary resource/cost estimates
  • Options identified (build, buy, use existing)

‚ùì Questions Explored

  • What are the must-have vs nice-to-have features?
  • Can existing systems meet 80% of the need?
  • What's the rough cost and timeline?
  • What's the expected business value/ROI?
‚úì

In Our Example

PMO works with HR to document requirements. They explore whether Dayforce's learning module can meet 80% of needs, or if a standalone LMS is required. A concept document is prepared.

üë•

Stage 4: Working Group Review

Domain experts collaborate and align on the approach

üë§ Who's Involved

  • People & Money Working Group members
  • HR representatives
  • Finance representatives
  • ITS leads

üìã What Happens

  • Concept presented to Working Group
  • Discussion on alignment with roadmap
  • Cross-department impacts considered
  • Prioritization against other initiatives
  • Recommendation developed

‚ùì Questions Discussed

  • How does this fit our People & Money roadmap?
  • Are there dependencies on other projects?
  • Should this be prioritized now or later?
  • Who else should be involved?
‚úì

In Our Example

The People & Money Working Group agrees the LMS is important. They recommend exploring the Dayforce learning module first, with a fallback to standalone options. They endorse moving to architecture review.

üîç

Stage 5: TSA-Team Review

Technical Standards & Architecture team ensures fit

üë§ Who's Involved

  • Technology architects
  • Security specialists
  • Data/integration experts
  • BRM and PMO

üìã What Happens

  • Solution options reviewed for architecture fit
  • Security requirements identified
  • Integration points mapped
  • Technical standards applied
  • Sustainability assessed

‚ùì Questions Evaluated

  • Does this fit our target architecture?
  • What are the security/privacy implications?
  • How will it integrate with existing systems?
  • Can we support this long-term?
‚úì

In Our Example

TSA-Team confirms that leveraging Dayforce's learning module aligns with the "extend existing investments" principle. If standalone is needed, they specify cloud hosting requirements and SSO integration needs.

Stage 6: D-DAT Governance Board

Final approval and prioritization decision

üë§ Who's Involved

  • CAO (Chair)
  • SMT representative
  • Selected Directors/Managers
  • Director ITS (Advisor)
  • PMO Manager (Advisor)

üìã What Happens

  • Project proposal presented
  • Strategic alignment confirmed
  • Resource/budget availability checked
  • Priority relative to other work decided
  • Go/no-go/defer decision made

‚ùì Board Considerations

  • Does this align with corporate priorities?
  • Do we have capacity to deliver this?
  • Is the timing right?
  • What's the opportunity cost?
‚úì

In Our Example

D-DAT Board approves the LMS project. Given resource constraints, they schedule it for Q3. If it required additional budget, it would go through the budget process first.
